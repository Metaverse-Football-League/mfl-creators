# Partnership Guidelines

> How MFL approaches creator partnerships. Operational reference for pitching, negotiating, and onboarding creators. For deal numbers (commission tiers, guarantee ranges), see `../mfl-wiki/knowledge-base/09-marketing/06-influencer-program.md`.

---

## Why Creators Matter for MFL

MFL isn't a typical game sponsorship. Three things make creators especially important:

1. **Trust**: The entry price is ~$80 for a digital asset. That's a higher bar than downloading a free game. People need to trust the product before spending, and they get that trust from creators they already follow.
2. **Complexity**: MFL is deep (scouting, tactics, marketplace, competitions). A landing page can't convey the experience. A creator streaming live can showcase, explain, and answer questions in real-time.
3. **Resale concept**: You can resell your club and players for real cash. This is unusual and needs a credible person to explain it.

When pitching to talent managers, lead with these three points to explain why MFL specifically needs creator partnerships (vs. just running ads).

## How to Position MFL to Creators

### The "Side Game" Framing

Position MFL as a complement to the creator's main content, not a replacement.

- Their main game (FIFA, FM, etc.) stays their primary focus and revenue
- MFL is an enjoyable side game that also generates recurring revenue via the affiliate deal
- This lowers the commitment bar: no new streaming schedule, no disruption to their existing audience

**Say**: "It's something on the side that they enjoy and that generates a bit of extra recurring revenue."

**Don't say**: "MFL should become a major part of your content."

### Streaming Integration

MFL content is integrated into existing streams, not a separate obligation.

- **After or during** their regular stream, not a new stream slot
- Natural transition: "Let me show you this game I've been playing"
- Target ~1 hour/week (1x60min or 2x30min, whatever feels natural for the creator)
- Viewers self-select: interested ones stay, others leave
- Unscripted gameplay: creator plays naturally (scouting, marketplace, matches)

**Say**: "You can just switch to MFL at the end of your stream for 20-30 minutes. It doesn't need to be a separate thing."

**Don't say**: "We need a dedicated MFL stream every week."

## Deal Pitch Talking Points

When explaining the deal model to creators or talent managers:

### The Affiliation + Guarantee Model

**Opener**: "We do an affiliation deal with a minimum guarantee."

**How to explain it**:
- The minimum guarantee is there to compensate the creator's work no matter what. Preparing streams, explaining the game, engaging with questions — that has value regardless of how many users sign up
- The affiliate commission is the upside. Up to 20% revenue share on referred users, scaling with performance
- Revenue share lasts up to one year per referred user
- If results are good, the creator naturally wants to invest more in MFL content. The model is self-reinforcing

**Key phrases**:
- "Security + upside" — guarantee covers the floor, affiliation rewards success
- "Up to 20%" — always say "up to", commission scales with volume
- "It's not their main revenue, but it's interesting recurring income on the side"

### What NOT to say
- Don't present the max commission tier (20%) as the default
- Don't promise "if it goes well we'll scale up" — people take that for granted and it's not guaranteed
- Don't compare deal sizes to big brands (Nike, EA) — frame MFL's approach as different, not smaller

## Long-Term Philosophy

MFL builds with creators. This isn't a one-shot sponsorship.

- "We want to know people in 5 years, not just for a week"
- We're a small company. We can't do big one-time payments like major brands. Instead we offer interesting long-term collaborations
- Recurring partnerships compound: creators develop genuine expertise and audience trust that short campaigns can't replicate
- Reference Nepenthez (1+ year partnership, still going strong) as proof this model works

**When a talent manager asks about budget**: "Yes, we have a budget. The exact amount depends on the creator's audience and reach, which is why I'd love to hop on a call to understand their community better."

## Try-Before-You-Commit Onboarding

Every potential partner should experience the game before any deal is signed.

1. **Offer a free club + small in-game budget** so the creator can discover MFL without spending anything
2. **No commitment at this stage** — genuinely want them to like the game first
3. **Authenticity check**: "We really want the content creators to like the game, to play it." If they don't enjoy it, the partnership won't work for either side
4. **Timing**: Mid-season mini-leagues for low-stakes discovery. Formal partnership start at the new season if timing works, but flexible

**Say**: "I'd love for [Creator] to try the game first and see if it's something they'd enjoy. We can set them up with a free club and a small budget to get started."

**Don't say**: "Let's sign a deal and then you can try the game."

## Quick Reference

| Topic | Guideline |
|-------|-----------|
| Positioning | Side game, not main game replacement |
| Streaming | Integrated into existing streams, ~1h/week |
| Deal model | Affiliation (up to 20%) + minimum guarantee |
| Duration | Long-term (years), not one-shot |
| Onboarding | Try the game first, no commitment |
| Authenticity | Non-negotiable — creator must genuinely enjoy MFL |
| Commission language | Always say "up to 20%", never present max as default |
