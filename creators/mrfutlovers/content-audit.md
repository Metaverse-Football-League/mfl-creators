## Observations

- **All YouTube videos are 100% MFL-dedicated.** Every video on the channel during the audit period is entirely about MFL. The script tagged 4 videos as "integration" because MFL appeared in the description rather than the title, but transcript review confirms all are fully dedicated MFL content. All have been reclassified accordingly.
- **Content is in German**, targeting the German-speaking MFL community. MrFutlovers is the only German-language MFL creator.
- **Community Team project is a recurring content pillar.** Multiple videos cover the community club (Ziegenschmiede), including recruitment, season reviews, and roster building. This drives engagement and viewer participation.
- **Notable gap in W03-W04, W06-W07, and now W10-W11.** MrFutlovers mentioned having a minor surgery (OP) around W03, which explains reduced output in January mid-month. W06-W07 (Feb 2-15) had zero activity. W10 (Mar 2-8) is another silent week with no content on any platform, extending into W11. This is the third multi-week content gap in the audit period.
- **Podcast ("MFL Deutschland") exists but is not captured on the main YouTube channel.** A tweet in W08 references a new podcast episode, and a W04 retweet references @mfldeutschland. These episodes appear to be published on a separate channel or platform and are not counted here.
- **X/Twitter engagement is modest** (typically 200-700 impressions, 0-4 likes per post). Most tweets link to YouTube videos. RTs of other MFL community members are also present but not counted as original posts.
- **Video views range from 62 to 123**, with an average around 85-100 views per video. Consistent but small audience.
