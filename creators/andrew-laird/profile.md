### Product Ideas Contributed
- **Against injuries feature**: Argues it removes strategic control and adds unwanted randomness — managers plan meticulously for matches and having key players suddenly unavailable undermines that strategy. Objects on gameplay frustration grounds, not economic ones.
- **Entry cost perception**: Notes that actual cost to get started is much lower than perceived (~$3-5 per player on secondary market, plus loans available). Believes more educational content is needed to communicate this to potential users.
- **Power creep investigation**: Interested in analyzing average OVRs per finishing position per league per season to evaluate community claims about power creep.
- **Club delegation for onboarding**: Suggests letting current users lend their clubs to new players for competitions — new users could try running an established club before buying their own. Sees it as a way to bring friends into the game.
- **ELO/instant match mode feedback**: Supportive of the concept but warns that rating systems can make new players feel automatically behind. Suggests separate leaderboards or matchmaking pools for newcomers. From a content perspective, instant matchmaking would be a major improvement — creators could spin up MFL streams anytime (like FIFA) instead of being locked to match schedules.
- **Preseason cup fatigue**: Felt there were too many preseason cups (Ramble, Wenderk, Yorkshire Tea) — didn't organize one himself because the market felt saturated. Good for existing players but doesn't help onboard new ones.
- **Off-season onboarding gap**: Signing up late in a season creates a dead period with nothing meaningful to do — risks losing new users who finally commit. No proposed solution but flagged as a real friction point in his referral experience.
