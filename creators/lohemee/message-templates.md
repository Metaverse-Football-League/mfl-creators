# Message Templates for Content Creators

> Ready-to-use message templates for common influencer communication scenarios.

---

## Overview

This document contains approved message templates for communicating with content creators. Adapt tone and details as needed while keeping core messaging consistent.

---

## Template 1: Recontact Email (Cold Lead)

**Use when**: Re-engaging a creator you spoke to months ago who went cold. Goal is to get a call, not to pitch.

```
Hey [name]! Hope you're doing well.

It's Mathurin, CEO at MFL. You had a great call with [person] back in [month year]. A lot has been going on on our side since then and I'd love to reconnect to explore a potential partnership.

[1-2 sentences of social proof / what's new. Keep it brief. E.g. mention an active creator partner, a milestone, etc.]

Would you be open to a quick call to catch up? Don't hesitate to suggest a few timeslots that work with you or to find a time here directly: https://calendly.com/mathurin-mfl/30min

I'm looking forward to it!
```

**Rules:**
- Self-identify with name and title
- Reference specific past interaction (who they spoke with, when)
- Do NOT pitch content angles or explain how MFL fits their channel. Save that for the call.
- Max 3 short paragraphs
- No sign-off name (email signature handles it)
- "I'm looking forward to it!" is for **first outreach and cold recontacts only**. Do NOT use it in follow-up messages to someone who hasn't replied. For follow-ups, end with the Calendly link or "Let me know!"

## Template 2: Deal Proposal

**Use when**: Proposing partnership terms to a creator who's already interested and in negotiation stage.

```
Hey [name]

[Personal context / re-engagement line if needed]

Here's what we usually do with our content partners:

- **Affiliate deal**: up to 20% revenue share, based on performance
- **Minimum guarantee**: ensures you get a base revenue even if affiliate performance is slow at first. You're creating content, so you get paid regardless of results
- **Deliverables**: [adjust to creator] 1 dedicated MFL video per week + 1 integration into your normal content (a quick 60-sec MFL overview in a [their main content] video) + 4 tweets per week

For the minimum guarantee, we could commit to €[amount]/month. Let me know if that works for you and I'll share more details on how the revenue share works.

The idea is you'd create MFL content the way you already create [their main content] — your style, your storytelling. We're not looking for ad reads, we want someone who actually plays and shows the game naturally.

Does that work for you? Happy to jump on a call if you'd rather talk it through.
```

## Template 3: Game Highlights (Post-Signing)

**Use when**: A creator has just signed and you want to share the key points they should highlight about MFL.

**Language**: Adapt to creator's language (French example below, English version follows)

### French Version

```
Hello !

Top, content de pouvoir bosser ensemble 🙌

Pour les points à mettre en avant auprès de ta commu :

- Tu crées et gères ton propre club : tu recrutes des joueurs, tu négocies leurs contrats avec des agents, tu construis ton effectif, tu participes à des compétitions
- Tu joues contre de vrais adversaires : chaque opposant est un vrai humain, pas d'IA. Tes décisions tactiques font vraiment la différence
- Tu fais progresser tes joueurs : ils évoluent grâce à l'entraînement et aux matchs, ils ont un potentiel caché à révéler
- Tu participes à des compétitions et gagnes des récompenses : saisons de 6 semaines, 10 divisions (de Flint à Diamond), coupes, tu peux monter dans les divisions et gagner de meilleures récompenses
- Tes joueurs et clubs ont une vraie valeur : tu peux les revendre pour du vrai cash

N'hésite pas si tu as des questions ou besoin de quoi que ce soit pour ton contenu !
```

### English Version

```
Hey!

Great, excited to work together 🙌

For the key points to highlight to your community:

- You create and manage your own club: you recruit players, negotiate contracts with agents, build your squad, compete in competitions
- You play against real opponents: every match is against a real human, no AI. Your tactical decisions genuinely make a difference
- You develop your players: they progress through training and matches, with hidden potential to unlock
- You compete and earn rewards: 6-week seasons, 10 divisions (Flint to Diamond), cups, climb the ranks for better rewards
- Your players and clubs have real value: you can sell them for real cash

Let me know if you have any questions or need anything for your content!
```

## Template 6: Integration Guidelines (Pre-Signing)

**Use when**: A creator has accepted the deal but wants clarity on what integrations should include before signing the contract.

```
Hey [name]! Thanks for the quick response, great to hear.

For the integrations, here's what we have in mind. Basically a 60-second segment within your existing videos (similar to how you'd do any sponsor segment). Here's what they should include:

- A brief mention/showcase of MFL (gameplay footage, the app, a match, your club, whatever fits naturally with the video)
- Your affiliate tracking link in the description + mentioned verbally or on screen
- A call to action for viewers to check out MFL

That's it. The idea is to keep it natural and fit your content style. You know your audience better than we do, so we're flexible on how you integrate it. We just want MFL to get a proper mention with the link.

Duration-wise, around 60 seconds is the target. No strict stopwatch on it, but it should be a real segment, not just a 5-second throwaway.

Let me know if you have any other questions. Happy to clarify if needed.
```

**Rules:**
- Keep it practical and concrete (bullet points for requirements)
- Emphasize flexibility on format — they know their audience best
- ~60 seconds as target duration, not a hard rule
- Casual tone, no overselling

---

## Key Reminders When Using Templates

1. **Personalize**: Don't copy-paste blindly. Adapt to the creator's style and your relationship.

2. **Check timing context**: Consider where we are in the season and adjust messaging accordingly (e.g., mid-season vs. season start).

3. **Match their language**: If they write casually, be casual. If more formal, adjust accordingly.

4. **Keep it short**: Creators are busy. Get to the point.
