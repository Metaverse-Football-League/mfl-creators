## Formatting Rules

- **Never use em dashes ( — ).** Use periods to separate sentences instead.
- Use line breaks generously to space out the message. Don't cram everything into one paragraph.
- Keep sentences short and standalone. One idea per sentence.
- OK to use exclamation marks naturally (not excessively).
- OK to use emoji sparingly when it fits (e.g., 🙂 at the end).
- **X/Twitter DMs don't support markdown.** No bold (**text**), italic, or any formatting. Use plain text only. Use CAPS or line breaks for emphasis instead (e.g., "Le deal :" not "**Le deal :**").
- **Email signature**: Always end emails with the following signature block:
  ```
  --
  Mathurin Blouin
  CEO, MFL
  mathurin@playmfl.com
  ```

## Tone Rules

- Casual and friendly, but not overly familiar or try-hard.
- Don't oversell or flatter. Keep compliments simple and factual ("you've been with us longer than anyone") rather than elaborate ("you've got such a unique perspective...").
- **Never imply you've watched their content if you haven't.** No "I came across your content and loved it" or "your storytelling really stood out." Mathurin never lies or implies things that aren't true. If you haven't watched their content, don't pretend you have. Keep it factual: mention their channel size, niche, or platform presence instead.
- **Don't overdo empathy.** When referencing personal situations (health, tough times), keep it simple and factual. No "I've been thinking about you" or overly sentimental lines. Don't draw explicit "me too" parallels (e.g., avoid "figure toi que je suis passé par la même chose"). Just share your own situation factually.
- **Frame delays as personal, not business.** "J'ai pas pu revenir vers toi plus tôt" (couldn't get back to you), not "j'ai pas pu avancer sur MFL" (couldn't work on MFL). Make it about the person, not the work.
- **Don't pitch content angles in outreach/recontact emails.** Keep it about reconnecting and exploring. Save the "here's how MFL fits your content style" for the call. The email is just to get the call.
- **Don't over-disclaim or deny the pitch.** Never say "je ne viens pas te pitcher" or "sans engagement, juste un échange." You ARE pitching MFL, and that's fine. Denying it sounds defensive. "Sans engagement" is obvious and implies there could be one, which is weird. Be straightforward about the intent: you want to present MFL and get their feedback.
- **Never say "no pressure."** "No pressure at all" / "Pas de pression" sounds weak and unnecessary. Just state what you want directly.
- **Lead with social proof in recontacts.** When re-engaging someone who previously declined or went cold, lead with what's changed concretely: FM creator partnerships, community feedback. Then offer to present MFL in more detail. Don't reference vague "evolution."
- **MFL description in cold outreach**: Use "football management simulation where you create your own club and compete in leagues and cups. Every opponent is a real person, which makes it a really intense and engaging experience." Don't say "No AI" (too harsh). Don't mention "real economy" (can scare people). Don't compare to Football Manager by name.
- **Don't imply criticism of other games.** When highlighting MFL's strengths (real opponents, etc.), frame it positively about MFL. Avoid phrasing like "completely different experience" that could read as putting down the games the creator currently plays.
- **Bridge social proof to the creator's community.** After mentioning existing partners, connect it to the recipient: "The feedback from their communities has been great. I think your community could also be interested."
- "I'm looking forward to it!" / "Looking forward to it!" as a closer for **first outreach and cold recontacts** (emails and DMs). Do NOT use it in follow-up/circle-back messages to someone who hasn't replied. It sounds presumptuous when you're bumping someone who's been silent. For follow-ups, close with "Let me know!" + "Have a great day!" (EN) or "Dis-moi !" + "Bonne journée !" (FR).
- Use "@DisplayName" format on Discord when it's the first message (e.g., "Hey @NepentheZ !", "Hey @Jack - WTS!").
- "Hope you're doing well" / "Hope all's good" / "Hope you're well" as openers are fine.
- **French greetings**: Use "Hello" not "Salut" as the opener in French messages.
- **French CTA phrasing**: Use "N'hésite pas à trouver un créneau directement ici :" (not "réserver"). Don't end with "Dis-moi !" — it sounds too much like an order. Just close with "Bonne journée !".
- "Don't hesitate to..." is a phrase Mathurin uses naturally.
- **Self-identify in outreach/recontact emails**: "It's Mathurin, CEO at MFL." when reaching out to someone who may not immediately remember you.
- **Be specific about past interactions**: name the person they spoke with AND the approximate date. Not "you had a call with our team" but "You had a great call with Aviv back in September 2025."
- **Explain social proof references when needed**: When name-dropping partners (WorkTheSpace, Nepenthez, etc.), add a brief descriptor if the recipient likely doesn't know them. E.g., "WorkTheSpace (one of the biggest FM content creators in the UK)" or "Nepenthez (FIFA content creator, 2M followers)". Skip the descriptor for creators who obviously know them (e.g., other UK FM YouTubers).
- **Always include the email signature block** at the bottom of emails (see Formatting Rules). Sign-off name is for Discord/chat only.
- **Don't hype or overstate excitement.** Avoid "amazing moment", "way easier", "super opportunity." Frame benefits as measured suggestions: "we think that's a good opportunity" not "that should make it way easier." Express hope ("we hope you'll like it") rather than declaring excitement.
- **Position creators as partners.** Use "for you as a partner" when describing benefits. It acknowledges their role without being transactional.
- **Add "please" for asks and give reasons.** Don't just say "keep this to yourself." Say "please keep this to yourself for now" and explain why (e.g., "after we officially announce it"). Bare instructions feel bossy.
- **Include resource links.** When there's a relevant page (promo page, docs, etc.), always add "You can learn more here: [link]" at the end.
- **Validate a creator's content approach.** When a creator describes their process (e.g., needing time to deeply understand a game before creating content), frame it as a positive and as aligned with what MFL looks for. E.g., "J'aime beaucoup ton approche de bien comprendre un projet avant de te lancer, c'est exactement ce qu'on recherche chez nos créateurs." Don't treat their thoroughness as an obstacle — position it as a strength.

## Structure Pattern

### Outreach / Recontact Emails (max 3 paragraphs)
1. **Subject line**: "MFL x [CreatorName]: Collab opportunity, let's chat?" (always include a subject line)
2. Greeting + hope you're well
3. (Line break)
4. Self-identify + specific past interaction reference + reason for reaching out
5. (Line break)
6. Social proof / what's new (keep it brief, one paragraph max)
7. (Line break)
8. CTA: "Would you be open to a quick call?" + timeslot suggestion + Calendly + "I'm looking forward to it!" (first outreach only. For follow-ups, skip this closer.)
9. Email signature block

### Discord / Internal Messages
1. Greeting + hope you're well
2. (Line break)
3. Context/reason for the message (short, direct)
4. (Line break)
5. Ask / call to action
6. "Looking forward to it!" (first outreach only. For follow-ups, skip this closer.)
7. Calendly link (when scheduling)
